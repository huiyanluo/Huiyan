Huiyan is an interaction designer based in London, interested in how digital platforms shape the way people think, behave, and connect with each other. Her work explores the stories, interfaces, and systems behind everyday technologies, especially the ones that feel natural, friendly, or invisible.

She is curious about how technologies are often humanised, mythologised, and presented as caring or intelligent, while the labour, power structures, and infrastructures behind them remain hidden. Through research-led design, moving image, games, and interactive installations, she investigates these tensions and turns them into experiences that people can see, feel, and question.

While trying to discover and explain problems, Huiyan enjoys building worlds, situations, and interactions that invite audiences to look at familiar technologies from different perspectives. For her, research is not the final goal but a starting point for creating new ways of seeing and experiencing the systems that shape contemporary life.
